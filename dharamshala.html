Dharamshala

Dharamshala (Devanagari: धर्मशाला; ITRANS: Dharmashala; IAST: Dharmaśālā) is a Hindi word (derived from Sanskrit) that is a compound of dharma (धर्म) and shālā (शाला). Literally, "House or place of Dharma". Dharamshala is the winter capital of Himachal Pradesh, India, as well as the site of the Tibetan Government-in-exile. It serves as administrative headquarters of the Kangra district after being relocated from Kangra, a city located 18 km away from Dharamshala, in 1855. Dharamsala is located in the Kangra Valley, in the shadow of the Dhauladhar mountains.

Dharamshala is a starting point to a number of trekking trails that especially includes lead trekkers across Dhauladhar into the upper Ravi Valley and Chamba district. Apart from their spiritual side, the delightful vistas of Dhauladhar mountain ranges, magnificent conifer, pine and deodar forests, lush tea gardens and stunning hill sides which offers best opportunities for nature walks, picnics and adventurous treks. The city has been selected as one of a hundred in India to be developed as a smart city under Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi's flagship "Smart Cities Mission".

Near the upper Dharamshala, you will see beautiful little Lhasa, Mcloedganj which is a serene residence of his holiness Dalai Lama dotted with lush greenery and majestic hills. It’s not wrong to say that Mcloedganj has the most scenic landscapes that remain etched in your heart if it comes to the Dharamshala holiday tour. Near it you will also witness the gorgeous Kangra Valley, Triund and serene monasteries which attracts thousands of International as well as Indian tourists over the year. The reason why Mcloedganj is mentioned especially in this blog is that it’s the central hub of Tibetan community which beautifies the ambiance and culture of Dharamshala.
