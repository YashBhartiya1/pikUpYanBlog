Shimla is the capital and the largest city of the northern Indian state of Himachal Pradesh. Shimla city gets its name from Shyamala Mata, a fearless incarnation of the goddess Kali. In 1864, Shimla was declared as the summer capital of British India. After independence, the city became the capital of East Punjab and was later made the capital city of Himachal Pradesh. It is the principal commercial, cultural and educational centre of the state.

Popular Destinations

1. The Ridge

The Ridge road is a large open space, located in the center of Shimla.The Ridge is the hub of all cultural activities of Shimla. It is situated along the Mall Road, which is the famous shopping center of Shimla.The Ridge is famous for the various government functions and fairs that are held here.

2. Kufri

Famed as the winter sports capital, Kufri is situated at a height of 2,622 meters above sea level. Settled in the foothills of the Himalayas, Kufri is a picturesque tourist location popular for its trekking and hiking trails, nature parks, picnic spots, local culture, and food stalls. Completely snow-covered in winters, Kufri is a popular destination for skiing and ice skating.

3. Jakhu Temple

Set amidst the lush green background of the Shivalik hill ranges, the highest point in Shimla, the Jakhu Temple is an ancient site dedicated to the Hindu god - Hanuman. Jakhu Temple has the world's largest Hanuman statue, which is visible from most parts of Shimla.

Read More

4. Johnnie's Wax Museum

The first wax museum in Himachal Pradesh, Johnnie’s Wax Museum at the Willow Banks estate in Shimla, is an ideal attraction for tourists. With more than 15 life-size wax statues like Mahatma Gandhi, Paul Walker, Daniel Craig, Harry Potter, and more. It is the perfect place to visit with family and to take pictures with famous personalities.

5. Sankat Mochan Temple

Sankat Mochan Temple is a temple dedicated to Hindu deity Hanuman in Shimla. The temple is Shimla's second most visited Hanuman temple after Jakhu Temple. The temple is located at an altitude of 1,975 m above sea level on Kalka-Shimla highway. The natural forested environment attracts tourists.

6. Chadwick Falls

Plummeting down 100 metres, amidst the dense Glen Forest, the Chadwick Falls is an adventurous experience in Shimla. Frequented by picnickers, this waterfall is located 7 km away from the city. The best to visit Chadwick falls is during monsoons from June to September.

7. Kali Bari Temple

Kali Bari Mandir is a Hindu temple situated on Bantony Hill, Shimla. The temple is dedicated to fearsome reincarnation of Goddess Kali, known as Shyamala, after which the Shimla city is named. It is only 1 km from Shimla Old Bus Stand and 1.5 km from Shimla Railway Station.

8. Annadale

Annadale, also spelled as Annandale is a locality of Shimla city, in the Indian state of Himachal Pradesh. It is a flat valley bottom which includes the helipad as well as a golf course in the city. Annadale has also an army cantonment with an Army Heritage Museum. It is also known as the "Heart of Shimla".

9. Tara Devi Temple

Tara Devi temple is one of the most visited religious places in Shimla. It is installed at a height of 7200 feet above sea level and situated around 11 km away from Shimla city. Tara Goddess in Hinduism and Buddhism is second of the Ten Great Wisdoms called ‘Mahavidyas’ and is known to be a source of all energies.

10. Dorje Drak Monastery

Also known as TDAC Nyingmapa Monastery, Dorje Drak Monastery is one of the popular tourist spots in the region. Nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas, this architectural marvel is rich in history and has the prevalence of Tibetan Culture on display to attract tourists from all walks of life.

11. Indian Institute of Advanced Study (IIAS)

The Indian Institute of Advanced Study (IIAS) is a research institute located in Shimla, India. It was set up by the Ministry of Education, Government of India in 1964 and started functioning from 20 October 1965.

12. Green Valley Shimla

Also known as the Hassan Valley, Green Valley, a popular attraction in Himachal Pradesh, promises an unforgettable holiday experience. Surrounded by an undisturbed view of the verdant hills, deodar trees and pine forests, the Green Valley offers more of a laid-back atmosphere to enjoy the tranquil setting.
